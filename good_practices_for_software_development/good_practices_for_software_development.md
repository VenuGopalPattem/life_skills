# Reflection on Good Practices for Software Development

## Question 1

### Which points were new to you?

One thing that was new to me was the idea of **over-communicating**. I used to think communicating only when necessary was enough, but now I understand that keeping everyone informed early prevents confusion later.

Another new point for me was how important it is to **ask clear and structured questions**. Instead of simply saying something is not working, explaining what I tried and sharing screenshots makes it easier for others to help.

I also learned that **deep focus and managing distractions** is a skill that needs practice, especially while working remotely.

---

## Question 2

### Which area do you think you need to improve on? What are your ideas to make progress in that area?

I think I need to improve mainly in two areas: **communication clarity** and **maintaining deep focus while working**.

To improve communication, I plan to:

* Take proper notes during meetings
* Summarize requirements and share them with the team
* Ask clearer and more specific questions when I get stuck

To improve focus, I plan to:

* Keep my phone on silent during work hours
* Turn off unnecessary notifications
* Work in fixed time blocks without distractions

By improving these areas, I believe I can become a more reliable and professional software developer.
