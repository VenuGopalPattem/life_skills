
**Question 1: What are the activities you do that make you relax - Calm quadrant?**

> Activities that help me relax and enter the Calm quadrant include 
  - meditation
  -  deep breathing exercises
  -  listening to  music
  -  taking short walks 
  -  simply sitting quietly and giving my mind a rest.
These activities allow me to recover, recharge, and reduce stress after intense mental or physical activity.

**Question 2: When do you find getting into the Stress quadrant?**

> I find myself entering the Stress quadrant when I am learning something new, facing a tight deadline, or doing a challenging workout. Stress arises naturally in situations that push me out of my comfort zone. Experiencing this stress, when managed properly, helps me grow and improves my skills over time.

**Question 3: How do you understand if you are in the Excitement quadrant?**

> I can tell I am in the Excitement quadrant when I feel energized, engaged, and motivated by a task or activity. Unlike stress, excitement feels positive, drives me forward, and makes me eager to participate while still being manageable and not overwhelming.


**Question 4: Paraphrase the *Sleep is Your Superpower* video (brief points)**

* Sleep boosts memory, learning, and brain function.
* Lack of sleep harms the body, immune system, and increases health risks.
* Regular sleep is essential for overall health.

**Question 5: Ideas to sleep better**

* Maintain a **consistent sleep schedule**.
* Keep the bedroom **cool and dark**.
* Avoid **caffeine, alcohol, and long naps**.



**Question 6: Brain-Changing Benefits of Exercise :**

1. Improves mood immediately by releasing “happy” harmones.
2. Increases focus and attention for a few hours after a workout.
3. Enhances long-term memory by growing new brain cells in the brain.
4. Strengthens prefrontal cortex and hippocampus, protecting against aging and dementia.
5. Provides lasting energy and overall brain health benefits.

**Question 7: Steps to Exercise More:**

* Exercise 3–4 times a week for at least 30 minutes.
* Include aerobic activity (walking, stairs, power-vacuuming).
* Try different activities to stay motivated (dance, yoga, kickboxing).
